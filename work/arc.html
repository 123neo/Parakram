ABOUT TRIVIA JUDGING CRITERIA/FAQ

“What was magic yesterday is called technology today! Humans now not only think for themselves but make machines ‘think’ as well. This event is all about an autonomous bot built from scratch by participants, judged on its ability to perform usual tasks. It tests the level of precision in the end to end designing and fabrication of the bot, and intelligent use of sensors and coding.”

Rounds and details:

    This event will be conducted in three stages:
  • ROUND 1: The bot should follow a maze in shortest time to qualify for the second round. You may stop the bot at the finish point.
  • ROUND 2: The bot should follow a maze and then a continuous wall of height 400 mm in the minimum time. The wall will be single sided.
  • ROUND 3: The bot should follow the maze and then a left wall and after that a right wall, all in continuation. The wall arena will contain only white surface. Finish point will be a black spot of 30 cm diameter.

    • In all the three rounds, teams who will be able to stop the bot at the finish point and blinks an LED for 5 seconds, will be awarded bonus points.
    You can find the prototype of the Arena here.

General Guidelines for the Competition:

  • Maximum of 5 can participate in a team.
  • Team has to be present at the arena 10 minutes before game starts. Absence will result to direct disqualification of the team.
  • Participants are advised to bring their own extensions for power supply.
  • No extra time will be given in case of wire entanglement. Participants are suggested to use wireless power supply for bots.
  • Participants should not disassemble their bots until results are announced.
  • Judges decision will be final.
  • Practice arena will be provided to the participants before the event starts. Once the match get started, no second trial will be given to team.
  • Teams will be given a "Time Out" if they want anytime between the match. Time Out will be of 90 seconds and will be given only once, in which teams can not make changes in their codes. Only hardware changes are allowed.
  • Maximum 5 man handllings are allowed with assigned penalities.
  • Teams will be given a 2 minute calibration time on the main arena before match starts.

Judging criteria

             1000*k/t + (x1+x2+…..+xn) + (other penalties)

              X1, … Xn = man handellings (-10 points)

             Other penalties = -10 points

             t = total time taken to complete task

             K = 25

             NOTE: "Other penalities" will be told at the time of competition.
